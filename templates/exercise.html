Exercise Plans

Exercise Plan for Age Below 18

1. Cardio

Engage in at least 30 minutes of cardio exercises like:

  • Running or jogging
  • Cycling
  • Swimming
  • Jump rope

2. Strength Training

Perform strength exercises 2-3 times a week, such as:

  • Push-ups
  • Squats
  • Lunges
  • Planks

3. Flexibility and Balance

Incorporate flexibility exercises daily, including:

  • Yoga
  • Stretching routines
  • Balance exercises like standing on one foot

Exercise Plan for Age 18 to 50

1. Cardio

Engage in 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week, such as:

  • Hiking
  • Dancing
  • Rowing
  • Elliptical training

2. Strength Training

Perform strength exercises 3 times a week, focusing on different muscle groups each day:

  • Day 1: Chest and triceps
  • Day 2: Back and biceps
  • Day 3: Legs and shoulders

3. Flexibility and Core

Incorporate flexibility and core exercises 2-3 times a week, including:

  • Pilates
  • Dynamic stretching
  • Core exercises (planks, leg raises)

Example Week Plan

Monday: 45-minute hike + chest and triceps workout

Tuesday: 30 minutes of dancing + core exercises

Wednesday: Rest day with light stretching

Thursday: 30 minutes of rowing + back and biceps workout

Friday: 30-45 minute elliptical session + legs and shoulders workout

Saturday: Pilates session

Sunday: Rest day or active recovery (light walk or gentle yoga)

Exercise Plan for Age Above 50

1. Cardio

Engage in 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week, such as:

  • Walking
  • Swimming
  • Cycling
  • Water aerobics

2. Strength Training

Perform strength exercises 2 times a week, focusing on major muscle groups:

  • Light weight lifting
  • Resistance band exercises
  • Bodyweight exercises (wall push-ups, seated squats)

3. Flexibility and Balance

Incorporate flexibility and balance exercises 3 times a week, including:

  • Yoga
  • Tai Chi
  • Stretching routines

4. Rest and Recovery

Ensure adequate rest and recovery to prevent injury and promote overall health:

  • At least two rest days per week
  • Active recovery activities like gentle walking or stretching

Example Week Plan

Monday: 30-minute walk + light weight lifting

Tuesday: 30 minutes of swimming + stretching

Wednesday: Rest day with gentle yoga

Thursday: 30 minutes of cycling + resistance band exercises

Friday: 30-minute walk + Tai Chi session

Saturday: Water aerobics

Sunday: Rest day or active recovery (light walk or stretching)
